Privacy Policy

Your trust is paramount. This policy details our commitment to protecting your data.

Last Updated: October 12, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Madarsa Connect ("the App"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our application. This policy applies to all users, including Heads (Admins), Faculty (Teachers), and Students. By using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

To provide a functional and personalized experience, we may collect several types of information:

2.1. Data You Provide to Us

  • Personal Identification Information: When you register an account, we collect your full name, email address, phone number, date of birth, and gender.
  • Profile Information: You may voluntarily provide a profile photo to personalize your account.
  • Communication Data: If you contact us for support, we may collect the contents of your message and your contact details to resolve your query.

2.2. Data We Collect Automatically

  • Academic & Usage Information: We collect data related to your role, such as course details, attendance records, marks, quiz submissions, and fee payment history.
  • Device Information: We collect your device's Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token to send you essential push notifications regarding announcements, fee reminders, or new quizzes.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected data for various purposes:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain our App.
  • To manage your account and provide you with customer support.
  • To process transactions, such as fee payments.
  • To send you important administrative information, such as updates, security alerts, and support messages.
  • To monitor and analyze usage to improve the App's functionality and user experience.

4. Data Storage and Security

Your information is securely stored on Google Firebase, a trusted and robust cloud platform. We implement a variety of security measures, including data encryption and secure access protocols, to maintain the safety of your personal information. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personally identifiable information to outside parties. This does not include:

  • Within Your Institution: Information is shared based on user roles (e.g., a teacher can view their students' attendance, but not another teacher's).
  • Trusted Third Parties: We may share data with service providers who assist us in operating our App (like Google Firebase and payment gateways), so long as those parties agree to keep this information confidential.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

6. Children's Privacy

Our service is designed for educational institutions which may include children. We are in compliance with the requirements of child privacy policies. We do not knowingly collect any personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13 without verifiable parental or institutional consent. If you believe we have collected such information, please contact us immediately so we can take necessary steps.

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to access, update, or correct your personal information through your in-app profile settings. For complete data deletion requests, please contact your institution's administrator or reach out to us directly through our support channels.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: madarsaconnect.org@gmail.com

In the modern educational landscape, data is far more than just a collection of numbers and charts; it is the key to unlocking a deeper understanding of each student's unique academic journey. By continuously tracking key metrics such as attendance patterns, participation levels, and performance on quizzes, teachers can spot red flags long before they become critical issues. This insight allows teachers to reflect on their approach, experiment with new pedagogical strategies (like using more visual aids or interactive examples), and ultimately tailor their instruction to the actual comprehension levels of their students, leading to significantly better learning outcomes for the entire class.
